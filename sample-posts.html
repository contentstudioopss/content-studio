My client Sarah lost 14kg last year without a single meal plan. No calorie counting. No banned foods. No 5am workouts. What she did instead: She ate the same 8 meals on rotation. She walked 20 minutes after dinner. She stopped eating in front of Netflix. That's it. Nothing revolutionary. Here's what most people get wrong about fat loss: They look for the perfect plan when what actually works is the boring plan you'll actually stick to. Sarah had tried keto, intermittent fasting, and two different apps before working with me. All of them "worked" — for about 3 weeks. The problem wasn't the diet. If your plan requires willpower, discipline, and perfect conditions to work — it won't work. What's one health habit you've actually maintained for more than 3 months?
